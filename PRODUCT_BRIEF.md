# Qi Fu App PRD

## 1. Product Summary

`Qi Fu` is a ritual and reflection app inspired by the Chinese concept of `祈福`: making a wish, offering a prayer, seeking blessing, and returning with gratitude when the wish is fulfilled.

The product combines symbolic interaction, meditation, and gentle community support. Users choose a blessing path, connect with a charm, write an intention, complete a short ritual, and hang the wish onto a digital blessing tree. If they allow visibility, others can view the wish and send blessings. When the wish is fulfilled, the user returns to close the cycle with gratitude.

This should feel emotionally meaningful, visually calm, and culturally respectful, not novelty-driven.

## 2. Product Vision

Create a modern ritual product that helps people:

- name what they hope for
- pause and reflect before acting
- feel held by a symbolic and supportive space
- return with gratitude when something changes

The long-term vision is a category-defining ritual app that sits between journaling, meditation, symbolic spirituality, and social encouragement.

## 3. Product Thesis

People already make wishes, pray privately, journal intentions, and seek comfort during uncertain periods. Most tools serve only one part of that emotional experience. `Qi Fu` creates a full loop:

1. intention
2. ritual
3. remembrance
4. community blessing
5. gratitude and return

The product’s value is not just recording a wish. It is making the wish feel witnessed, held, and revisitable.

## 4. Goals

### User Goals

- Give users a beautiful, structured way to express hope, prayer, or intention.
- Make the act of posting a wish feel calm and ceremonial.
- Let users revisit wishes over time, rather than forgetting them immediately.
- Create a gentle social layer where support feels warm, not performative.
- Build a meaningful gratitude loop when wishes are fulfilled.

### Business Goals

- Validate demand for a ritual-reflection product with social engagement.
- Identify whether the blessing tree and return ritual drive retention.
- Test whether people prefer private, friend-only, or public wishes.
- Establish a foundation for premium content such as charm packs, meditation content, and tree themes.

## 5. Non-Goals for V1

- Deep theology or religious instruction
- Open public social networking with follower graphs
- Marketplace or gifting mechanics
- Highly realistic 3D asset production across all charms
- Android, iPad, and web desktop optimization beyond responsive support

## 6. Target Users

### Primary User

A reflective, emotionally expressive user who likes intention-setting, journaling, spirituality, meditation, or symbolic rituals. They may not identify with a formal religion, but they value meaning, timing, luck, support, and personal growth.

### Secondary User

A socially engaged user who enjoys reading public wishes, sending encouragement, and participating in seasonal rituals or themed community trees.

### Likely Early Adopter Profiles

- People who already use journaling, manifestation, tarot, astrology, or meditation apps
- Chinese and Chinese-diaspora users drawn to `祈福` symbolism
- Younger users who enjoy emotionally resonant, visually rich mobile experiences
- Users in transitional life moments: exams, healing, relationships, career shifts, family concerns

## 7. Jobs To Be Done

- When I feel uncertain, I want a meaningful way to express what I’m hoping for.
- When I make a wish, I want it to feel more intentional than typing a note into a blank journal.
- When I share something vulnerable, I want support without pressure or performance.
- When life changes, I want to come back and mark what happened, so the experience feels complete.

## 8. Core Experience Principles

- `Ritual over utility`: every key action should feel intentional, not transactional.
- `Calm over clutter`: the app should reduce noise, not add it.
- `Support over performance`: social features should encourage tenderness, not popularity contests.
- `Symbolism with clarity`: cultural symbolism should be explained simply and respectfully.
- `Closure matters`: the return-with-gratitude moment is a core differentiator, not a side feature.

## 9. Core User Flow

1. User opens the app and sees blessing paths.
2. User selects a blessing category.
3. User sees the associated charm and its symbolic meaning.
4. User interacts with the charm.
5. User writes a wish, prayer, or intention.
6. User optionally adds a target date or time horizon.
7. User chooses privacy: private, friends-only, or public blessings.
8. User completes a short meditation or focus ritual.
9. The wish is hung on the blessing tree.
10. If shared, others can tap the wish and send blessings.
11. The user revisits the wish later through reminders or archive prompts.
12. When fulfilled, the user marks it complete and leaves a gratitude note.

## 10. Blessing Categories

- Wealth and prosperity (`招财`, `财运`)
- Health and wellbeing (`平安`, `健康`)
- Good luck and protection (`好运`, `顺利`)
- Love and relationships (`姻缘`, `爱情`)
- Family harmony (`家庭`, `和睦`)
- Career and study (`事业`, `学业`)
- Travel and safe journeys (`出行平安`)
- Inner peace and meditation (`静心`, `修心`)

## 11. Charms and Symbolism

Each category maps to one or more charms, each with a short story and symbolic meaning. Final art can evolve later, but the product should establish a stable symbolic system early.

Example charm set:

- Fortune pouch
- Jade pendant
- Red wish ribbon
- Gold ingot
- Lotus charm
- Guardian knot
- Peach blossom token
- Wooden prayer plaque

Each charm should include:

- charm name
- associated category
- short symbolic explanation
- optional visual interaction
- optional ritual cue, such as a sound, glow, or motion style

## 12. Feature Set

### 12.1 Blessing Creation

- Browse blessing categories
- Select a charm
- Read a short charm meaning
- Enter wish title and description
- Optionally choose a time horizon or target date
- Optionally tag the wish, for example healing, exam, family, business
- Choose privacy settings

### 12.2 Ritual Moment

- Short meditation or focus timer
- Candle, incense, breathing, or sound cues
- Haptic or visual confirmation when a wish is released
- Ritual transition from writing state to hanging state

### 12.3 Blessing Tree

- Personal tree for the user’s wishes
- Community tree for public wishes
- Wish objects visualized as charms, ribbons, plaques, or leaves
- Tap any public wish to reveal details
- Return state for fulfilled wishes

### 12.4 Social Blessings

- Send blessing action
- Optional supportive messages
- Optional public comments, likely limited or moderated in later versions
- Follow a wish if the creator allows updates

### 12.5 Gratitude and Return

- Mark a wish fulfilled
- Add a gratitude message
- Archive fulfilled wishes
- Show a visual change on the tree when the cycle is complete

## 13. V1 MVP Scope

### In Scope

- Account creation or lightweight sign-in
- 8 blessing categories
- 1 charm per category
- Charm meaning view
- Wish creation with title, description, time horizon, and privacy
- Short meditation ritual
- Personal and public tree views
- Tap into public wish details
- Send blessing to public wishes

- Basic archive

### Out of Scope

- Full real-time chat
- Friend graph or DMs

- User-generated charm uploads

## 16. Social and Community Rules

This product can easily become noisy or emotionally unsafe if social features are not constrained.

V1 social rules should be:

- blessings first, comments second
- no visible like counts framed as competition
- no ranking users by popularity
- public wishes are opt-in only
- anonymous or pseudonymous sharing should be considered
- reporting, hiding, and moderation tools are required for public content

## 17. Privacy and Trust

Privacy is foundational, because wishes can be deeply personal.

Each wish should support:

- `Private`: only visible to the creator
- `Friends only`: visible to approved social graph or invite group later
- `Public blessings`: visible in community tree

Trust requirements:

- clear visibility choice before posting
- ability to edit or unpublish shared wishes
- easy delete flow
- content reporting for public wishes
- moderation review path for abuse, harassment, or explicit content

## 18. Tone and Style Direction

### Brand Mood

Modern Chinese, calm, refined, and emotionally warm. The app should feel ceremonial and contemporary at the same time.

### Visual Cues

- Porcelain, vermilion, jade, ink, parchment, and gold accents
- Delicate textures inspired by silk, carved wood, paper fibers, or ink wash
- Gentle atmospheric lighting and motion
- Negative space used to create stillness

### Interaction Mood

- Soft transitions
- Slightly slowed ritual moments
- Graceful reveal states
- No loud gamification cues

## 19. Recommended Platform Path

### Recommendation

Start with a mobile-first web app or cross-platform mobile build to validate the loop quickly, then move toward native iOS polish if retention is promising.

### Why Web or Cross-Platform First

- faster iteration
- easier sharing of public wishes
- lower release overhead
- easier experimentation with onboarding and visual flows

### Why iOS Later

- stronger haptics
- more premium ritual feel
- tighter control over animation and audio
- better fit for short, repeat mobile sessions

## 20. Success Metrics

### Activation

- percent of new users who create their first wish
- percent of new users who complete meditation before hanging
- time to first hung wish

### Engagement

- wishes created per active user
- percent of users who revisit a wish within 7 days
- percent of public wishes that receive at least one blessing

### Retention

- D1, D7, D30 retention
- return rate to archive or fulfilled wish flow
- repeat ritual completion rate

### Social Health

- blessings sent per public wish
- report rate on public content
- ratio of private vs public wishes

## 21. Key Risks

- The product may feel shallow if the ritual moment is too weak.
- Public wishes may introduce moderation and privacy problems early.
- The product may become visually beautiful but behaviorally thin.
- The experience may become culturally vague if symbolism is aesthetic but not meaningful.
- Rich 3D or animation work can increase cost before the core loop is validated.

## 22. Open Questions

- Should public wishes show usernames, pseudonyms, or stay anonymous by default?
- Should public blessings allow free text in V1, or only preset supportive phrases?
- How strong should the spiritual framing be versus wellness framing?
- Should the primary tree be personal first, community first, or a blend?
- Does the return ritual drive enough emotional payoff to become the product’s signature feature?
